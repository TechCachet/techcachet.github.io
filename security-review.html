Security Overview

Customer-facing summary for Marketplace and security reviewers. It is not a certification, penetration test, or legal document. See the Privacy Policy for full detail.

Architecture

  • Atlassian Forge: App logic runs on Forge; configuration and operational state use Forge app storage (Key-Value Store) on Atlassian infrastructure.
  • No Tech Cache app servers for core Jira processing as described here—we do not operate long-lived servers that replace Forge for routine issue/SLA flows.
  • Jira data (issues, comments, users per permissions) stays under your and Atlassian’s control; the app reads/writes through installed scopes.

Optional integrations (customer-controlled)

When you configure Slack (webhook, bot token, channel, DMs) or HTTPS webhooks to automation tools (e.g. Zapier, Make), notification payloads leave Atlassian for those endpoints over HTTPS only when the destination hostname is on the Forge external.fetch allowlist in the app’s manifest.yml—not arbitrary customer URLs. Content may include issue keys, SLA text, and user-related fields consistent with your templates.

  • Secrets (e.g. Slack bot token) are stored in Forge storage; treat as sensitive customer configuration.
  • Email-related APIs may be used for Slack matching when enabled—see Privacy Policy (read:email-address:jira where declared).

What we do not claim

  • No SOC 2, ISO 27001, or other attestation unless we publish explicit evidence.
  • No guarantee that the app meets your industry or internal security baseline—your admins must review configuration and Atlassian settings.

Operational practices (high level)

  • Design goal: avoid logging secrets and unnecessary personal data in production—verify in your build.
  • Tokens surfaced to browsers are masked where the product implements masking; admins can clear stored tokens in configuration when supported.
